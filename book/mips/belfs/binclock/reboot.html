14.4. Booting the system

The first step is to merge the new program into the initial ram disk. One one of the great properties of the gz archive is that concatenating two archives creates an archive. Thus: 


cat ${CLFS}/build/initramfs ${CLFS}/build/binclock.gz > ${CLFS}/build/initrd.gz 



  
  gnome-terminal -x \
    qemu-system-mipsel -kernel ${CLFS}/boot/clfskernel-2.6.38.2 \
    -initrd ${CLFS}/build/initrd.gz -M malta -redir tcp:5555::80 -m 128 -nographic -append "rdinit=/sbin/init console=ttyS0"

When the boot is complete, the embedded CLFS system is ready for use!
